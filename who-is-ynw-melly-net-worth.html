RAPPER YNW Melly rose to fame in the late 2010s for his mixtape releases, but is now on trial accused of a double murder.

Melly, real name Jamel Demons, is facing the death sentence, if convicted.

Who is YNW Melly?

YNW Melly is an American rapper hailing from Gifford, Florida.

He was born on May 1, 1999, and is the son of Jamie Demons-King.

The YNW acronym in his stage name stands for Young N**** World or Young New Wave.

His first project was an EP titled Collect Call, which was released in 2017 while he was still serving time in prison.

Following Collect Call, Melly released his debut mixtape named I Am You.

Since then, he has released three additional albums and worked with several stars, including Kanye West, A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie, Lil Keed, Lil Durk, Lil Baby, and Juice WRLD.

Melly is arguably best known for the songs Murder on My Mind, Mixed Personalities, Suicidal, and 223's.

Melly's second mixtape, We All Shine, was distributed in January 2019.

Why is YNW Melly on trial?

YNW Melly was arrested in February 2019 for the alleged drive-by shooting murders of Christopher Thomas Jr and Anthony Williams, known as YNW Juvy and YNW Sakchaser respectively.

Both men were members of the rap group YNW Melly is a part of, and were killed in October 2018.

He has been in Broward main jail since then despite calls for his release during the coronavirus pandemic.

Ahead of the trial, the court judge lowered the threshold for a death sentence if YNW is convicted.

The death penalty can now be recommended by the jury on an 8-4 split rather than a unanimous vote.
