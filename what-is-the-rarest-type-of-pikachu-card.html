What is the Rarest Type of Pikachu Card?

 Posted on February 24, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  674 words  |  Aldo Pusey

In the captivating world of Pokémon card collecting, the allure of owning rare and valuable cards has become a pursuit for many enthusiasts.

Among the vast array of Pokémon cards, Pikachu cards stand out as some of the most sought-after and prized possessions in the realm of trading card games.

Here, we will explore the rarest types of Pikachu cards, delving into their unique attributes, historical significance, and the extraordinary value they hold in the eyes of collectors and investors.

The Illustrious Pikachu Illustrator Card

The Pikachu Illustrator card is undoubtedly the Holy Grail of Pokémon card collecting, known for its unparalleled rarity and exceptional value.

This illustrious card originated from a series of art contests conducted by the esteemed Japanese comic magazine, CoroCoro Comic, in 1997. Winners of these contests were bestowed with the exclusive Pikachu Illustrator card, featuring an enchanting artwork of Pikachu brandishing a paintbrush, with captivating illustrations adorning the background.

The scarcity of this card is evident from the fact that only 39 official copies were awarded to the contest winners, making it an exceedingly rare commodity in the Pokémon card universe.

The rarity and desirability of the Pikachu Illustrator card were further amplified when two additional copies resurfaced in 2020, elevating the total count to a mere 41 existent copies.

This scarcity has accentuated its value, with the card fetching astronomical prices in the market. Notably, the most expensive transaction involving this card was facilitated by renowned influencer Logan Paul, who acquired the highest graded copy for a staggering $5.275 million in July 2021, marking it as the most expensive private Pokémon card sale in history. The Enigmatic Trophy Pikachu No. 2 Trainer Card

Another enigmatic and highly sought-after Pikachu card is the Trophy Pikachu No. 2 Trainer card, renowned for its exceptional rarity and exorbitant value.

This prestigious card was exclusively distributed to the winners of the 1999 Lizardon Mega Battle regional tournament in Japan, symbolizing an emblem of triumph and prestige for its recipients.

The limited availability of this card is evidenced by the existence of only four reported copies, making it one of the most elusive and scarce Pokémon cards in existence.

The incredible rarity of the Trophy Pikachu No. 2 Trainer card has translated into substantial value, with a perfect PSA Gem Mint 10 graded copy being sold for an astounding $444,000 in September 2023, firmly establishing its position as one of the rarest and most valuable Pikachu cards.
