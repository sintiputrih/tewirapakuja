What is the Professional Career of Walker Hayes?

On December 27, 1979, Charles Edgar Walker Hayes was born in Mobile, Alabama. As a teenager, he was interested in both music and sports. Before moving to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Hayes went on to acquire a music degree at Birmingham-Southern College. In an effort to get entry into the country music industry, Hayes and his wife moved to Nashville in 2005. He signed with Mercury Records Nashville after getting a job writing songs for a Nashville publishing house, but was soon fired and transferred to Capitol Records Nashville.

His debut song, “Pants,” was made available in 2010. For the week ending September 18, 2010, it debuted on the Hot Country Songs charts at No. 60. Kyle Ward of Roughstock scored the song 3.5 out of 5 stars. In December 2010, Hayes premiered the song’s music video. On a 19 Kids and Counting episode, Hayes also performed a song that he had written about Jill and her future husband, Derick. Also, On Capitol Records’ Reason to Rhyme album, “Pants” and a second single, “Why Wait for Summer,” both appeared. On “Dirty Side,” from Colt Ford’s 2014 album Thanks for Listening, Hayes co-wrote the song and provided guest vocals. He also contributed to Rodney Atkins’ late-2014 single “Eat Sleep Love You Repeat.”

In 2016, Hayes agreed to a publishing and production deal with Shane McAnally and SMACK/RareSpark. As a result, two extended plays, 8Tracks Vol. 1 and 8Tracks Vol. 2, were published. In August 2018, Hayes released a single with the title “90’s Country,” which makes multiple references to 1990s-era country song names.On June 4, 2021, Hayes released the EP Country Stuff. One of its tracks, “Fancy Like,” became a TikTok viral smash, peaked at number one on the Hot Country Songs list and third on the US Billboard Hot 100, making it Hayes’ highest-charting release to date.
