Burt Bacharach Net Worth: How Much Money The Late Composer Make?

 Posted on March 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  602 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The composer, who began his career in the 1950s, was able to amass a sizeable sum of money. Aside from being a pianist, he also achieved success as a record producer, music arranger, actor, and film soundtrack composer. Here is all the information we have on his earnings and net worth.

Burt Bacharach Net Worth

Burt Bacharach had a net worth of $160 million at the time of his passing in February 2023, according to the website Celebrity Net Worth. His lengthy and fruitful career as a composer is where the majority of his revenues came from. He collaborated with lyricist Hal David on dozens of notable tunes from the 1950s to the 1980s.

Bacharach was born in Kansas City in 1928 to a mother who was a painter and a newspaper columnist. He began taking piano lessons when he was a young child, but his preference was jazz. He received his musical education at the Mannes School of Music, McGill University in Montreal, and the Music Academy of the West in Montecito. He was enlisted for two years in the US army in 1950.

Songwriting Career

When Bacharach met and collaborated with Hal David in 1957, his career received a significant boost. The duo created a series of notable songs over the following several years, including The Story of My Life, which peaked at number one on the US country chart. Magic Moments, Make it Easy on Yourself, and I’ll Never Fall in Love Again are a few of their other well-known compositions.

Bacharach released Hit Maker, his debut solo album, in 1965. On the UK charts, Burt Bacharach Plays His Hits peaked at number three. He and David fell out in 1973, yet he still put out solo records. He collaborated with his wife, the songwriter Carole Bayer Sager, in the 1980s.

The duo collaborated on popular songs including That’s What Friends Are For, On My Own, and Arthur’s Theme (Best That You Can Do). Bacharach made a comeback in 2016 and wrote the original score for the movie A Boy Called Po after a protracted absence. He collaborated with Daniel Tashian in 2020 to create the Grammy-nominated album Blue Umbrella.

Acting Appearances

Through the 1960s and 1970s, Bacharach also made guest appearances on a few television programmes and in a few movies. He played the lead role in the NBC musicals An Evening and Another Evening with Burt Bacharach. Additionally, he appeared in Martini & Rossi ads and had cameo parts in the Austin Powers movies.

The musician also made a sizable real estate investment. He spent $2.5 million on a home in the Pacific Palisades neighbourhood of Los Angeles in 1998. Currently, the property is estimated to be worth $12 to $15 million. Bacharach spent $5.5 million on another house in 2015. The home is located in California’s beach town of Del Mar.
