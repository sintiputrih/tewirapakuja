FAQS

Is Garth Brooks a songwriter that writes his own songs?

Garth Brooks is a songwriter. However, he mostly uses songs written by others on his albums. “We Shall Be Free,” “Much Too Young (To Be This Damn Old),” “If Tomorrow Never Comes,” “Not Counting You,” “Unanswered Prayers,” “The Thunder Rolls,” and “The River” are among the songs he helped write.

Where did Garth Brooks work as a boot salesman?

Cowboy Boots. Brooks moved to Nashville in 1987, where he worked as the manager of a cowboy boot store, was paid to sing on demo CDs by other composers, and began pitching to publishers and record labels.

Who was Garth’s first wife, and what happened to her?

On May 24, 1986, Garth Brooks married his college love Sandy Mahl in Oklahoma. After 15 years of marriage, the couple split up in 1999 and divorced in December 2001 after finding Nashville and developing a famous music career.
