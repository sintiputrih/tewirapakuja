Is Magpie Murders Based On A True Story?

Anthony Horowitz, a British author, published his debut book in the Susan Ryeland series, Magpie Murders, in 2016. The narrative uses a story inside a story structure and centers on the murder of a mystery writer.

The book has been made into a six-part drama series and has been translated into numerous other languages.

Fans who are worried about how the plot will work on television should rest assured knowing that the script is in the greatest possible hands.

“Magpie Murders is my most popular book, and adapting it wasn’t simple. But I believe the outcome is a wholly original narrative that will equally charm and surprise viewers, according to Horowitz.

On October 6, 2016, Orion published Magpie Murders in hardcover and e-book editions for the first time in the UK. Both Orion and BrillianceAudio simultaneously released an audiobook adaption read by Allan Corduner and Samantha Bond.

Magpie Murders Plot Summary

Horowitz created the idea for the Magpie Murders during the 1997 debut of the first season of Midsomer Murders.

According to his statements, he wanted the book to be “a sort of thesis on the entire genre of murder mystery writing, and to be more than just a murder mystery fiction. How these books are created; how the authors develop their ideas.”

The next year, Harper and HarperAudio released the book in hardback, e-book, and audiobook formats in the United States. In the UK and the US, paperback editions were issued in 2017 and 2018, respectively.

The book was translated into several languages in the years that followed, including Korean and Japanese (2018).

According to a Deadline report from July 2020, the novel will be turned into a six-part drama series for PBS’ Masterpiece, which would air in the US and on BritBox in the UK.

The script will be written by Horowitz, and it will be produced by Masterpiece, Jill Green, and Eleventh Hour Films.

Lesley Manville takes the lead role of Susan, and Tim McMullan was cast to play Atticus Pünd when Timothy Spall withdrew from the production owing to schedule conflicts.

The cast of the show also includes Daniel Mays, Alexandros Logothetis, Jude Hill, Matthew Beard, and Claire Rushbrook.
